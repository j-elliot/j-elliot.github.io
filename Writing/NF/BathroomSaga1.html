"Bathroom Saga 1"
30 November, 2020

I replumbed pretty much all of my father's house yesterday. I used PEX and it ended up being 700 in materials, which is a bit more than I would have liked, but it's done, it's done well, and it got rid of essentially all of the brittle old CPVC that was there before. I say essentially all, because I didn't replace every termination, so there are a few inches of CPVC between most fixtures and the Sharkbite coupling that transitions to the PEX. As things get renovated, those last few inches will also be replaced and updated.
My dad is a good sport about the things I do. I've made his house my laboratory for trying out new things and learning new skills. The replumb was a step in the preparation of a bathroom renovation. The trouble there is that after we pulled up the old linoleum, what we were left with was beautiful hardwood. Not ideal for a bathroom, but too nice to just ignore. So what can we do? Well, we can use it, work really hard to waterproof it, and make the theme of the bathroom a more natural, woodsy tone than I had originally considered.
Here are some skills I'm going to have to develop for this project: I need to learn to build a window. From glass and wood. I need to waterproof wood floor. I need to learn how to build a living wall. I need to build a rough cabinet. I need to design and build a water control closet. [1]
