Process Versus Goal
'Goal oriented' is a common and well lauded concept, and for good reason. In planning a project, the most important task is defining clear goals and carefully tracking your progress towards reaching them. But exclusive focus on narrowly defined goals can create dehumanizing and dangerous work environments. It's process that considers things like safety, personal development, and human dignity. Process oriented thinking is the decision to do something the right way, even though that isn't the fastest, easiest, cheapest way. Every shortcut you don't take is putting the process above the goal.
On the other hand, “A good plan, violently executed now, is better than a perfect plan next week.” (Quoted from Patton: War as I Knew It). Especially for entrepreneurs, the quick, dirty solution is often the only viable option. 'Goal oriented' is a virtue for good reason, but as with many aspects of our lives and careers, we can't adopt one perspective to the exclusion of all others. Balancing goals and process is one of many tightropes we have to traverse.
